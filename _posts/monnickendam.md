It's been a while that I wrote a blog post about [cycling](/2022/cycling/). As I mentioned at that post, we went to Monnickendam. It was a very delightful journey for us. For the curious minds, I'm gonna add the route that we followed below.

Since its our first long road trip, we wanted to take the easy path which will make us feel more confident. We don't have any repair kit so we decided to prepare a route which we will be able to reach any repairshop within 30 minute range. 30 minute, because this is something that we can walk comfortably even if we feel a bit tired. That's how we found Monnickendam. And our journey started.

While cycling along with the Noordhollandsch canale, we saw too many farms that we even though maybe we can bought something for us in the future. It's really nice to have a chance to buy directly from producer. After a 20-30 minute later we arrived to [Broek in Waterland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broek_in_Waterland). According to documentary that we watched in [Monnickendam Museum](https://www.despeeltoren.nl/index-english/), this place is one of the wealthiest village in North Holland. They were lots of beatiful houses inside the village that we see while passing along. But even that image was enough for us to add it to our bucket list. Before closing this section, you can refill your bottles at [this](https://google.com/maps/@52.4339023,4.9974757,3a,75y,156.04h,90t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1slHa0pYQSk_-SDJD4QhHLKA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) place while commuting through Monnickendam.

After 15-20 minutes later, we finally arrived to [The Grote Kerk](http://www.grotekerkmonnickendam.nl/EN/). The church was built into historical center of the Monickendam, near the N247 road. The church itself very humble but remarkably huge and impressive. If you like to take a look before visiting, it's interesting but you can use Google Street inside the church, here is the [link](https://google.com/maps/@52.4568344,5.032859,3a,75y,200.12h,45.42t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1sAF1QipO00ISrjqsjPCWJVTlRTrxeIsvuNDDN7cfP8pLm!2e10!3e2!7i13312!8i6656). Don't forget to collect info card from the front desk.

Then head through [tourist info](https://goo.gl/maps/bfHMYYnowyz7dzPj8). There was two lovely lady at desk, who provided us a map and tell us the hidden gems of the Monnickendam. Since we loaded with information, next stop for us was [Monnickendam Museum](https://goo.gl/maps/hxjFN374MXPEPdde8). It may seem like a small, ordinary museum to you but museum has the oldest carillon of the world. It nearly a 500 years old but still working.
